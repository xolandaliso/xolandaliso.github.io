ABOUT ME.

academic career. interests.

I was born and raised in a small town called Dutywa in the province of the Eastern Cape, where I did my primary and high school education, matriculating in 2014. In 2015, I enrolled for a degree in Physical Science at University of the Western Cape, graduating in 2017. I mainly did Physics because of my Grade 12 Physical Science teacher. Her teaching strategy really made me curios to take the subject further and learn more about it.

In 2015 at the beginning of my undergraduate career I got offered a 4-year scholarship from SARAO(SKA SA). The SKA scholarship targets students with oustanding marks from matric and that are interested in studying the wonders of the universe, Astronomy in short.

In 2018, I enrolled for Honors in Astrophysics under the Natiaonal Astrophysics and Space Science Program (NASSP). at University of Cape Town (UCT), where I was selected to be amomgst the top achievers graduating with Cum Laude.

Recently I have submitted my Master's thesis in Astrophysics at UWC which I started in June 2019, following an intense M.Sc coursework at UCT. For my M.Sc project I focused on quantifying the amount of dark matter in NGC 1512/1510, using telescope data observed using the Australia Telescope Compact Array (ATCA). For this project I used Python programming laguage to do data visualization, analysis, etc, making me have 4 years of solid and understanding of Python ;).

Pursuing a degree in Astronomy has given me a good taste of other various discplines such as the use of statistical models in the big data era, application of machine learning techniques in Astronomy, etc. As such building machine learning predictive models is one of my main interests.

In addition to my strong python skills I have also taken time to upskill myself by self-learning some of the important programming languages such as Java, R, SQL mainly for databases and also the markup languages: HTML and CSS maily for styling.
